DVD RENTAL

What I did

In this project, I conducted a comprehensive data analysis of a DVD rental company's extensive transaction and inventory data. This analysis encompassed six key film categories: Animation, Music, Family, Children, Classics, and Comedy. Utilizing PostgreSQL for efficient data management and querying, I analyzed and compared data initially provided in CSV format to extract valuable business insights. My analysis focused on uncovering critical performance indicators, including:

  • Identifying Top Revenue Generators: Developed and executed SQL queries to determine the highest paying customers on a monthly and annual basis, enabling targeted loyalty programs or marketing initiatives.
  • Pinpointing High-Demand Genres: Analyzed rental order patterns using PostgreSQL to identify the film category with the highest number of rentals, providing crucial information for inventory stocking and promotional focus.
  • Genre Representation: Calculated the distribution of movies across the six categories to understand the current inventory balance and identify potential gaps or overstocking in specific genres.

The results of this analysis offer actionable insights for the DVD rental company to optimize customer engagement, strategically manage their film inventory, and potentially enhance their service offerings based on data-driven understanding of customer preferences and rental trends.
